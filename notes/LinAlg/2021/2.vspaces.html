  1. $\forall x \in \E:\;\; 1_\mathbb K \cdot x=x$
  2. $\forall \alpha \in \K, \forall \beta \in \K, \forall x \in \E:\;\; (\alpha+\beta) \cdot x=\alpha \cdot x+\beta \cdot x$
  3. $\forall \alpha \in \K, \forall \beta \in \K, \forall x \in \E:\;\;(\alpha \beta) \cdot x=\alpha \cdot(\beta \cdot x)$
  4. $\forall \alpha \in \K, \forall x \in \E, \forall y \in \E:\;\; \alpha \cdot(x+y)=\alpha \cdot x+\alpha \cdot y$

យើងនឹងបង្ហាញថា $(\E, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល ហើយផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់បន្ថែមលក្ខណៈ (i.) ដល់ (iv.) ។

  1. បង្ហាញថា $(\E, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល:
    • លក្ខណៈផ្ដុំ៖ យក $a,b,c \in \E$ គេបាន \begin{eqnarray*} a+(b+c) \;\;&=&\;\; x_a+i y_a + \bigl(x_b+i y_b + x_c + i y_c\bigr)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; x_a+i y_a + \Bigl((x_b+x_c) + i (y_b + y_c)\Bigr)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \bigl(x_a+(x_b+x_c)\bigr) + i \bigl(y_a+(y_b + y_c)\bigr)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \bigl((x_a+x_b)+x_c\bigr) + i \bigl((y_a+y_b) + y_c\bigr)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \Bigl((x_a+x_b) + i (y_a + y_b)\Bigr) + x_c+i y_c \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \bigl(x_a+i y_a + x_b + i y_b\bigr) + x_c+i y_c \\ \;\;&=&\;\; (a+b) +c \end{eqnarray*}
    • លក្ខណៈត្រឡប់៖ យក $a,b \in \E$ គេបាន \begin{eqnarray*} a+b \;\;&=&\;\; x_a+i y_a + x_b+i y_b\\ \;\;&=&\;\; (x_a+x_b) + i (y_a + y_b)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; (x_b+x_a) + i (y_b + y_a)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; x_b + i y_b + x_a +i y_a\\ \;\;&=&\;\; b+a \end{eqnarray*}
  • មានធាតុណឺត៖ សន្មត $e\in \E$ ជាធាតុណឺត ។ គេបាន គ្រប់ $a\in \E$: \begin{eqnarray*} e+a \;\;&=&\;\; a \\ \Leftrightarrow\quad (x_e+iy_e) + (x_a+i y_a) \;\;&=&\;\; x_a+iy_a\\ \Leftrightarrow\quad (x_e+x_a) + i (y_e+y_a) \;\;&=&\;\; x_a + i y_a \end{eqnarray*} ដូច្នេះ \[ \begin{cases} x_e+x_a = x_a & \\ y_e+y_a = y_a \end{cases} \;\; \Longleftrightarrow \quad \begin{cases} x_e+x_a = 0+x_a & \\ y_e+y_a = 0+y_a \end{cases} \] ដូច្នេះ $x_e = 0, y_e=0$ ។
  • ធាតុច្រាស៖ យក $a\in \E$ និងតាង $a^{-1} = \alpha +i\beta$ ជាធាតុច្រាសរបស់ $a$ ។ គេបាន \begin{eqnarray*} a+a^{-1} \;\;&=&\;\; e \\ \Leftrightarrow\quad (x_a+iy_a) + (\alpha+i \beta) \;\;&=&\;\; 0+i 0\\ \Leftrightarrow\quad (x_a+\alpha) + i (y_a+\beta) \;\;&=&\;\; 0 + i 0 \end{eqnarray*} ដូច្នេះ \[ \begin{cases} x_a+\alpha = 0 & \\ y_a+\beta = 0& \end{cases} \;\; \Longleftrightarrow \quad \begin{cases} x_a+\alpha = x_a + (-x_a) & \\ y_a+\beta = y_a + (-y_a) & \end{cases} \] ដូច្នេះ $\alpha = -x_a, \beta = -y_a$ ។

  2. $\forall \alpha \in \K, \forall \beta \in \K, \forall z \in \E:\;\; (\alpha+\beta) \cdot z=\alpha \cdot z+\beta \cdot z$? \begin{eqnarray*} (\alpha+\beta) \cdot z \;\;&=&\;\; (\alpha+\beta) \cdot (x+iy) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; (\alpha+\beta) x+ (\alpha+\beta) iy\\ \;\;&=&\;\; (\alpha x + \beta x) + (i\alpha y + i\beta y)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha (x + iy) + \beta (x + i y)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha\cdot z + \beta \cdot z \end{eqnarray*}
  1. $\forall \alpha \in \K, \forall \beta \in \K, \forall z\in \E:\;\;(\alpha \beta) \cdot z=\alpha \cdot(\beta \cdot z)$ ? \begin{eqnarray*} (\alpha\beta) \cdot z \;\;&=&\;\; (\alpha\beta) \cdot (x+iy) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; (\alpha\beta) x+ (\alpha\beta) iy\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha(\beta x)+ i\alpha (\beta y)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha (\beta x+ i\beta y)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha\Bigl(\beta ( x + iy)\Bigr)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha\cdot (\beta\cdot z) \end{eqnarray*}
  2. $\forall \alpha \in \K, \forall z_1 \in \E, \forall z_2 \in \E:\;\; \alpha \cdot(z_1+z_2)=\alpha \cdot z_1+\alpha \cdot z_2$ ? \begin{eqnarray*} \alpha \cdot (z_1+z_2) \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha \Bigl((x_1+iy_1) + (x_2+iy_2)\Bigr) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha \Bigl((x_1+x_2) + i(y_1+y_2)\Bigr) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha (x_1+x_2) + i \alpha(y_1+y_2) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha x_1 + \alpha x_2 + i \alpha y_1 + i \alpha y_2 \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha (x_1+i y_1) + \alpha (x_2 + i y_2)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \alpha\cdot z_1 + \alpha \cdot z_2 \end{eqnarray*}

ដូច្នេះ $(\mathbb C, +, \cdot)$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័រកំណត់លើកាយ $\mathbb R$ ។

ចម្លើយ. យក $\E=\mathbb{R}^n$ និង $\K=\mathbb{R}$ ។

យើងនឹងបង្ហាញថា $(\E, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល ហើយផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់បន្ថែមលក្ខណៈ (i.) ដល់ (iv.) ។

  1. បង្ហាញថា $(\E, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល:
    • លក្ខណៈបំណិត (Closure) ចំពោះ $(+)$: បើយកវ៉ិចទ័រ \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{K}^n\) នោះគេបាន \(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}\) ត្រូវតែនៅក្នុងសំណុំ \(\mathbb{K}^n\) ព្រោះ \(\mathbf{u} = (u_1, u_2, \ldots, u_n)\) និង \(\mathbf{v} = (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) ដែលផលបូកកំណត់ដោយ៖ \[ \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = (u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2, \ldots, u_n + v_n). \] ដោយផលបូកកូអរដោនេនីមួយៗ \(u_i + v_i\) ជារបស់ \(\mathbb{K}\) ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(i\) គេបាន \(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{K}^n\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \(\mathbb{K}^n\) is closed.
    • លក្ខណៈផ្ដុំចំពោះ $(+)$៖ យក \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w} \in \mathbb{K}^n\) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} (\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) + \mathbf{w}\;\; &=&\;\; \bigl(u_1 + v_1 , \ldots, u_n + v_n \bigr) + (w_1, \ldots, w_n)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \Bigl((u_1 + v_1) + w_1, (u_2 + v_2) + w_2, \ldots, (u_n + v_n) + w_n\Bigr) \end{eqnarray*} ហើយ \[ \mathbf{u} + (\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{w}) = \Bigl(u_1 + (v_1 + w_1), u_2 + (v_2 + w_2), \ldots, u_n + (v_n + w_n)\Bigr) \] តាមលក្ខណៈផ្ដុំរបស់ប្រមាណវិធីបូកក្នុងកាយ \(\mathbb{K}\) គេបាន $(u_i + v_i) + w_i = u_i + (v_i + w_i)$ ចំពោះគ្រប់ $i$ ។ ដូច្នេះ ប្រមាណវិធីបូក $(+)$ ក្នុង $ \mathbb{K}^n$ មានលក្ខណៈផ្ដុំ ។
  • លក្ខណៈត្រឡប់ចំពោះ $(+)$៖ យក \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{K}^n\) គេបាន \begin{eqnarray*} \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \;\; &=& \;\;(u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2, \ldots, u_n + v_n) \\ \;\; &=& \;\; (v_1 + u_1, v_2 + u_2, \ldots, v_n + u_n). \end{eqnarray*} ព្រោះ \(u_i + v_i = v_i + u_i\) ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(i\) (លក្ខណៈត្រឡប់ចំពោះ $(+)$ ក្នុងកាយ \(\mathbb{K}\)) នោះគេបាន \(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{u}\) ។
  • ធាតុណឺតចំពោះ $(+)$៖ មានធាតុណឺត \(\mathbf{0} = (0, 0, \ldots, 0) \in \mathbb{K}^n\) ចំពោះ $(+)$ ព្រោះគ្រប់ \(\mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{K}^n\) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{0} &=& (v_1 + 0, v_2 + 0, \ldots, v_n + 0) \\ &=& (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n) \\ &=& \mathbf{v} \end{eqnarray*}
  • ធាតុច្រាសចំពោះ $(+)$៖ គ្រប់ \(\mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{K}^n\) មាន \(-\mathbf{v} = (-v_1, -v_2, \ldots, -v_n)\) ដែល : \begin{eqnarray*} \mathbf{v} + (-\mathbf{v}) &=& (v_1 + (-v_1), v_2 + (-v_2), \ldots, v_n + (-v_n)) \\ &=& (0, 0, \ldots, 0) \\ &=& \mathbf{0} \end{eqnarray*} ដូច្នេះ \(-\mathbf{v}\) ជាធាតុច្រាសរបស់ \(\mathbf{v}\) ចំពោះ $(+)$ ។
We conclude that \((\mathbb{K}^n, +)\) is indeed an abelian group. \(\blacksquare\)
  • លក្ខណៈបំបែក៖ ធៀបនឹងប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង $(+)$ (Distributive property w.r.t vector addition)៖
    យក \(\mathbf{u} = (u_1, u_2, \ldots, u_n)\) និង \(\mathbf{v} = (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) ។ គេបាន៖ \[ \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = (u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2, \ldots, u_n + v_n). \] ដោយគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ $a$ គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} a(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) &=& a(u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2, \ldots, u_n + v_n) \\ &=& \Bigl(a(u_1 + v_1), a(u_2 + v_2), \ldots, a(u_n + v_n)\Bigr) \end{eqnarray*} ដោយប្រើលក្ខណៈបំបែករបស់ប្រមាណវិធីគុណក្នុងកាយ \(\mathbb{K}\) គេបាន \(a(u_i + v_i) = a u_i + a v_i\) ចំពោះគ្រប់ $i$។ ដូច្នេះ គេបាន៖ \[a(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) = a \cdot \mathbf{u} + a \cdot \mathbf{v}\]
    ធៀបនឹងប្រមាណវិធីបូកស្កាលែរ (Distributive property w.r.t scalar addition)៖ យក \(\mathbf{v} = (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) និង $a, b\in \K$។ គុណនឹង $(a+b)$ គេបាន ៖ \[ (a + b) \cdot \mathbf{v} = (a + b)(v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n) = \Bigl((a + b)v_1, (a + b)v_2, \ldots, (a + b)v_n\Bigr) \] ដោយប្រើលក្ខណៈបំបែកក្នុង \(\mathbb{K}\) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \Bigl((a + b)v_1, (a + b)v_2, \ldots, (a + b)v_n\Bigr) \;\;&=&\;\; (av_1 + bv_1, av_2 + bv_2, \ldots, av_n + bv_n)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; (a v_1, a v_2, \ldots, a v_n) + (b v_1, b v_2, \ldots, b v_n) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; a \cdot \mathbf{v} + b \cdot \mathbf{v} \end{eqnarray*} ដូច្នេះ \[(a + b) \cdot \mathbf{v} = a \cdot \mathbf{v} + b \cdot \mathbf{v}\]
  • លក្ខណៈផ្ដុំចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ (Associativity of scalar multiplication):
    យក \(\mathbf{v} = (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) និង $a, b \in \K$ ។ គេបាន \(b \cdot \mathbf{v}\): \[ b \cdot \mathbf{v} = (b v_1, b v_2, \ldots, b v_n). \] បន្ទាប់មកគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ \(a\) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} a\bigl(b \cdot \mathbf{v}\bigr) \;\;&=&\;\; a\bigl(b v_1, b v_2, \ldots, b v_n\bigr) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \Bigl(a(b v_1), a(b v_2), \ldots, a(b v_n)\Bigr) \end{eqnarray*} តាមលក្ខណៈផ្ដុំរបស់ប្រមាណវិធីគុណក្នុងកាយ \(\mathbb{K}\) គេបាន៖ \[ \Bigl(a(b v_1), a(b v_2), \ldots, a(b v_n) \Bigr)= \Bigl((ab) v_1, (ab) v_2, \ldots, (ab) v_n \Bigr) \] ដូច្នេះ \[a(b \cdot \mathbf{v}) = (ab) \cdot \mathbf{v}\]
  • ធាតុណឺតចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ (Identity element of scalar multiplication):
    យក \(\mathbf{v} = (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) ។ ពិនិត្យ $1 \cdot \mathbf{v} $ : \[ 1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 1(v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n) = (1 \cdot v_1, 1 \cdot v_2, \ldots, 1 \cdot v_n). \] ដោយ $1$ ជាធាតុណឺតចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីគុណក្នុង \(\mathbb{K}\) គេបាន៖ \[ (1 \cdot v_1, 1 \cdot v_2, \ldots, 1 \cdot v_n) = (v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n) = \mathbf{v} \] ដូច្នេះ \[1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v}\]

ដូច្នេះ ប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង $(+)$ និងប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $(\cdot)$ កំណត់លើ \(\mathbb{K}^n\) ផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់លក្ខខណ្ឌចាំបាច់របស់លំហវ៉ិចទ័រ ។ i.e., $(\mathbb{K}^n, +, \cdot)$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័រកំណត់លើកាយ $\K = \mathbb R$ ។

យក $\K$ ជាកាយ និងកំណត់ $\K[x]$ ជាកងពហុធាមានមេគុណកំណត់ក្នុងកាយ $\K$ ។ បង្ហាញថា $\K[x]$ គឺជា $\K$-vs ចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $(\cdot)$ ៖ $$ \begin{array}{rccl} \cdot: & \K \times \K[x] & \longrightarrow & \K[x] \\ &\Bigl(\lambda, P(x)\Bigr) & \longmapsto &\lambda \cdot P(x)=\sum_{i=0}^n\left(\lambda a_i\right) x^i \end{array} $$ និងប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង $(+)$ ៖ $$ \begin{array}{rccl} +: & \K[x] \times \K[x] & \longrightarrow & \K[x] \\ &\Bigl(P(x), Q(x)\Bigr) & \longmapsto & P(x) + Q(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{\max (n,m)}\left(a_i + b_i\right) x^i \end{array} $$ ដែល $P(x)=\sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i$ និង $Q(x)=\sum_{i=0}^m b_i x^i$ ។

ចម្លើយ. ${}$ យក $\K = \R$ ។ ដើម្បីបង្ហាញថា $\R[x]$ ជា $\R$-vs គេត្រូវផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់ស្វ័យសត្យរបស់លំហវ៉ិចទ័រខាងក្រោម៖

តាង $\begin{cases} P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i &\\ Q(x)=\sum_{i=0}^m b_i x^i & \\ R(x) = \sum_{i=0}^p c_i x^i & \end{cases}$ ជាពហុធានៅក្នុង $\R[x]$ ដែល \( a_i, b_i, c_i \in \R \) ជាមេគុណរបស់ពហុធា និងមានដឺក្រេ \( n, m, p \) រៀងគ្នា និងយក $\lambda, \beta \in\R$ ។

  1. បំណិត (closure):
    • ចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ (Under scalar multiplication):
      គ្រប់ $\lambda \in \R$ និង $P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i \in \R[x]$ គេបាន៖ \[ \lambda \cdot P(x) = \lambda\cdot \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i = \sum_{i=0}^n (\lambda a_i) x^i \] ដោយមេគុណនីមួយៗ $\lambda a_i \in \K$ នោះគេបាន $\lambda \cdot P(x)\in \R[x]$ ។
    • ចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង (Under addition):
      យក $P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i$ និង $Q(x) = \sum_{i=0}^m b_i x^i$ ជាពហុធាពីរក្នុង $\R[x]$ ។ គេបាន៖ $$P(x) + Q(x) \;\; =\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} (a_i + b_i) x^i$$ ដោយមេគុណនីមួយៗ $(a_i + b_i) \in \R$ នោះគេបាន $ P(x) + Q(x) \in \R[x]$ ។
  1. លក្ខណៈរបស់ផលគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ (Scalar multiplication properties):
    • $1 \cdot P(x) \; \overset{?}{=}\; P(x)$
      គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} 1 \cdot P(x) \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^n (1 \cdot a_i) x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i = P(x) \end{eqnarray*}
    • $\lambda\Bigl(\mu \cdot P(x)\Bigr) \;\overset{?}{=}\; (\lambda\mu) \cdot P(x)$
      គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \lambda\Bigl(\mu \cdot P(x)\Bigr) \;\;&=&\;\; \lambda\left(\sum_{i=0}^n (\mu a_i) x^i\right) \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^n \lambda(\mu a_i) x^i\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^n (\lambda\mu) a_i x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; (\lambda\mu) \cdot P(x) \end{eqnarray*}
  1. លក្ខណៈបំបែក (Distributive properties):
    • $\lambda\Bigl(P(x) + Q(x)\Bigr) \;\overset{?}{=}\; \lambda \cdot P(x) + \lambda \cdot Q(x)$:
      គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \lambda\Bigl(P(x) + Q(x)\Bigr) \;\;&=&\;\; \lambda\left(\sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} (a_i + b_i) x^i\right)\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} \lambda(a_i + b_i) x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} (\lambda a_i + \lambda b_i) x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^n (\lambda a_i) x^i + \sum_{i=0}^m (\lambda b_i) x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \lambda \cdot P(x) + \lambda \cdot Q(x) \end{eqnarray*}
    • $(\lambda + \mu) \cdot P(x) \;\overset{?}{=}\; \lambda \cdot P(x) + \mu\cdot P(x)$:
      គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} (\lambda + \mu) \cdot P(x) \;\;&=&\;\;\sum_{i=0}^n \bigl((\lambda + \mu)a_i\bigr) x^i\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^n (\lambda a_i + \mu a_i) x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \lambda \cdot P(x) + \mu \cdot P(x) \end{eqnarray*}
  1. លក្ខណៈផ្ដុំចំពោះ $+$ (Associativity of addition):
    យក \( P(x), Q(x), R(x) \in \K[x] \) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \Bigl(P(x) + Q(x)\Bigr) + R(x) \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} (a_i + b_i) x^i + \sum_{i=0}^{p} c_i x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(\max(n,m),p)} ((a_i + b_i) + c_i) x^i \end{eqnarray*} ម្យ៉ាងទៀត \begin{eqnarray*} P(x) + \Bigl(Q(x) + R(x)\Bigr) \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{n} a_i x^i + \sum_{i=0}^{\max(m,p)} (b_i + c_i) x^i \\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n, \max(m,p))} \left( a_i + (b_i + c_i) \right) x^i \end{eqnarray*} ក្នុងផលបូកខាងលើ យក \(a_i, b_i \) ឬ \( c_i = 0 \) ក្នុងករណីសន្ទស្សន៍ $i \gt n, m, p$ រៀងគ្នា ។ ដោយប្រើលក្ខណៈផ្ដុំរបស់ $(+)$ នៅក្នុងកាយ $\R$: \[ (a_i + b_i) + c_i = a_i + (b_i + c_i) \] ដូច្នេះ គេបាន \[ (P(x) + Q(x)) + R(x) = P(x) + (Q(x) + R(x)) \]
  1. លក្ខណៈត្រឡប់ចំពោះ $+$ (Commutativity of addition):
    គ្រប់ពហុធា \( P(x), Q(x) \in \K[x] \) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} P(x) + Q(x) \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} (a_i + b_i) x^i\\ \;\;&=&\;\; \sum_{i=0}^{\max(n,m)} (b_i + a_i) x^i\\ \;\;&=&\;\; Q(x) + P(x) \end{eqnarray*} ដោយប្រើលក្ខណៈត្រឡប់នៅក្នុង $\R$ គឺ : $a_i + b_i = b_i + a_i$។ ដូច្នេះ \[ P(x) + Q(x) = Q(x) + P(x) \]
  2. ធាតុណឺត (Existence of the zero vector):
    ពហុធាសូន្យ \( 0(x) = 0 \) គឺជាវ៉ិចទ័រសូន្យក្នុង \( \R[x] \) ព្រោះគ្រប់មេគុណរបស់ពហុធាសូន្យ គឺស្មើសូន្យ ។ ដូច្នេះ គ្រប់ \( P(x) \in \R[x] \) គេបាន៖ \[ P(x) + 0 = \sum_{i=0}^n (a_i + 0) x^i = P(x) \] ដូច្នេះ មាន \(0(x)\in \K[x] \) ជាធាតុណឺតចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង $(+)$។
  3. ធាតុច្រាស (Existence of additive inverses):
    គ្រប់ពហុធា \( P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i \) គេមានពហុធា \((-1)\cdot P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n (-a_i) x^i = -P(x)\) គឺជាចម្រាសរបស់ $P(x)$ ចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីបូក ព្រោះ \[ P(x) + (-P(x)) = \sum_{i=0}^n (a_i - a_i) x^i = 0 \]

ដោយ \( \R[x] \) ផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់គ្រប់លក្ខណៈរបស់លំហវ៉ិចទ័រ ដូច្នេះ \( (\K[x], +, \cdot )\) គឺជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័រកំណត់លើកាយ \( \R \) ។

យក $\E$ ជា $\K$-vs និងកំណតសំណុំ $A\neq \varnothing$ ។ គេតាង $\E^A$ ជាសំណុំនៃគ្រប់អនុគមន៍ទាំងអស់ កំណត់ពី $A$ ទៅ $\E$ ។ ចូរបង្ហាញថាសំណុំ $\E^A$ គឺជា $\K$-vs ដែលមានប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ ៖ $$ \begin{array}{rccl} \cdot: &\K \times \E^A & \longrightarrow & \E^A \\ &(\lambda, f) &\longmapsto &\lambda \cdot f \end{array} $$ ចំពោះ $f, g\in \E^A$ និង $\lambda\in \K$ គេកំណត់ ៖
  1. $\lambda \cdot f$ គឺជាអនុគមន៍ពី $A$ ទៅ $\E$ កំណត់ដោយ៖ $$ \forall x \in A:\; (\lambda \cdot f)(x)=\lambda \cdot f(x) $$
  2. បើ $f$ និង $g$ ជាអនុគមន៍ពី $A$ ទៅ $\E$ នោះគេបាន $f+g$ ក៏ជាអនុគមន៍ពី $A\to\E$ កំណត់ដោយ៖ $$ \forall x \in A:\; (f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x) $$

ចម្លើយ. ↩

ចម្លើយ. ${}$ យក \( \E^A = \Bigl\{f \mid f : A \to V\Bigr\}\) ជាសំណុំនៃគ្រប់អនុគមន៍កំណត់ពី \(A\) ទៅ \(\E\)
ដែល $A\neq \varnothing$, $\E$ ជា $\R$-vs ។ គេកំណត់៖
  • ប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង \((+)\) របស់អនុគមន៍ \(f, g \in \E^A\) ត្រង់ចំណុចនីមួយៗ ៖ \[ (f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x), \quad \forall x \in A \]
  • ប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ \((\cdot)\) (scalar multiplication) របស់អនុគមន៍ \(f \in \E^A\) ជាមួយនឹងស្កាលែរ \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\) ត្រង់ចំណុចនីមួយៗ ៖ \[ (\alpha \cdot f)(x) = \alpha \cdot f(x), \quad \forall x \in A \]
i.e., ប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង \(+ : \E^A \times \E^A \to \E^A\) និងប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ \(\cdot : \mathbb{R} \times \E^A \to \E^A\) ។

យើងនឹងបង្ហាញថា $(\E^A, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល ហើយផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់បន្ថែមលក្ខណៈ (i.) ដល់ (iv.) ។

  1. បង្ហាញថា $(\E^A, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល:
    • Closure: យក \(f, g \in \E^A\) គេបានផលបូក \(f + g\) កំណត់ដោយ \[(f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)\] ដោយ \(f(x), g(x) \in \E\) ហើយ \(\E\) គឺជា $\R$-vs នោះគេបាន៖ \(f(x) + g(x) \in \E\) គ្រប់ \(x \in A\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \[f + g \in \E^A\]
    • លក្ខណៈផ្ដុំ៖ គ្រប់ \(f, g, h \in \E^A\) គេបាន៖ \[\Bigl[(f + g) + h\Bigr](x) = (f + g)(x) + h(x) = \Bigl(f(x) + g(x)\Bigr) + h(x)\] ដោយ $(+)$ ក្នុង $\E$ មានលក្ខណៈផ្ដុំ គេបាន៖ \[\Bigl[f(x) + g(x)\Bigr] + h(x) = f(x) + \Big[g(x) + h(x)\Bigr]\] ដូច្នេះ \(\Bigl[(f + g) + h\Bigr](x) = \Big[f + (g + h)\Bigr](x)\) គ្រប់ \(x \in A\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \[(f + g) + h = f + (g + h)\]
    • លក្ខណៈត្រឡប់៖ ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(f, g \in \E^A\), \((f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)\) ។ ដោយ $(+)$ របស់ \(\E\) មានលក្ខណៈត្រឡប់ គេបាន \(f(x) + g(x) = g(x) + f(x)\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \((f + g)(x) = (g + f)(x)\) គ្រប់ \(x \in A\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \[f + g = g + f\]
    • មានធាតុណឺតចំពោះ $(+)$ : យក \(\mathbf 0 \in \E^A\) ជាអនុគមន៍កំណត់ដោយ \[\mathbf 0(x) = 0_V, \quad \forall \; x \in A\] ដែល \(0_V\) ជា identity ចំពោះ $(+)$ ក្នុង \(\E\) ។ ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(f \in \E^A\) គេបាន៖ \[(f + \mathbf 0)(x) = f(x) + \mathbf 0(x) = f(x) + 0_V = f(x)\] ដូច្នេះ \[f + \mathbf 0 = f\]
    • ចម្រាសចំពោះគ្រប់ធាតុរបស់ $\E^A$ : គ្រប់ \(f \in \E^A\) គេកំណត់ \[(-f) \in \E^A :\; (-f)(x) =-f(x), \quad \forall \; x \in A\] ដែល \(-f(x)\) គឺជាចម្រាសរបស់ \(f(x)\) ក្នុង \(\E\) ចំពោះ $(+)$ ។
      ដូច្នេះ \[\Bigl(f + (-f)\Bigr)(x) = f(x) + \Bigl(-f(x)\Bigr) = f(x) -f(x) = 0_V\] ដូច្នេះ \[f + (-f) = 0\]
    ដូច្នេះ $(\E^A, +)$ ជាក្រុមអាបែល ។
  1. លក្ខណៈចំពោះផលគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ៖
    • លក្ខណៈចុះសម្រុងរវាងផលគុណនឹងស្កាលែរជាមួយនឹងផលគុណក្នុងកាយ៖ គ្រប់ \(\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{R}\) និង \(f \in \E^A\) គេបាន \[\Bigl((\alpha \beta) \cdot f\Bigr)(x) = (\alpha \beta) \cdot f(x)\] ដោយផលគុណនឹងស្កាលែរក្នុងលំហ \(\E\) ផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់៖ \[\Bigl(\alpha \beta\Bigr) \cdot v = \alpha \cdot \Bigl(\beta \cdot v\Bigr),\quad \forall\; v \in \E\] គេបាន \[(\alpha \beta) \cdot f(x) = \alpha \cdot \Bigl(\beta \cdot f(x)\Bigr) = \alpha \cdot \Bigl((\beta \cdot f) (x)\Bigr)= \Bigl(\alpha \cdot (\beta \cdot f)\Bigr)(x)\] ដូច្នេះ \[(\alpha \beta) \cdot f = \alpha \cdot (\beta \cdot f)\]
    • ធាតុណឺតរបស់ប្រមាណវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ :
      គ្រប់ \(f \in \E^A\) គេបាន \[(1 \cdot f)(a) = 1 \cdot f(a)\] ដោយ \(1 \cdot v = v\) គ្រប់ \(v \in \E\) នោះគេបាន \[1 \cdot f(a) = f(a) \implies 1 \cdot f = f\]
    • លក្ខណៈបំបែករបស់ប្រមាណវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ និងប្រមាណវិធីបូកវ៉ិចទ័រ : គ្រប់ \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\) និងគ្រប់ \(f, g \in \E^A\) : \[ \Bigl(\alpha \cdot (f + g)\Bigr)(x) = \alpha \cdot (f + g)(x) = \alpha \cdot \Bigl(f(x) + g(x)\Bigr)\] ដោយវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់៖ \(\alpha \cdot (v + w) = \alpha \cdot v + \alpha \cdot w\) គ្រប់ \(v, w \in \E\) នោះគេបាន៖ \[\alpha \cdot \Bigl(f(x) + g(x)\Bigr) =\alpha \cdot f(x) + \alpha \cdot g(x)\] ដូច្នេះ \(\Bigl(\alpha \cdot (f + g)\Bigr)(x) = \Bigl((\alpha \cdot f) + (\alpha \cdot g)\Bigr)(x)\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \[\alpha \cdot (f + g) = \alpha \cdot f + \alpha \cdot g\]
    • លក្ខណៈបំបែករបស់ប្រមាណវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ លើប្រមាណវិធីបូកក្នុងកាយ : គ្រប់ \(\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{R}\) និង \(f \in \E^A\) គេបាន៖ \[ \Bigl((\alpha + \beta) \cdot f\Bigr)(x) = (\alpha + \beta) \cdot f(x) \] ដោយវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរក្នុង \(\E\) ផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់ \((\alpha + \beta) \cdot v = \alpha \cdot v + \beta \cdot v\) គ្រប់ \(v \in \E\) គេបាន៖ \[ (\alpha + \beta) \cdot f(x) = \alpha \cdot f(x) + \beta \cdot f(x) \] ដូច្នេះ \( \Bigl((\alpha + \beta) \cdot f\Bigr)(x) = \Bigl((\alpha \cdot f) + (\beta \cdot f)\Bigr)(x)\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \[ (\alpha + \beta) \cdot f = \alpha \cdot f + \beta \cdot f \]

ដូច្នេះ $(\E^A, +, \cdot)$ គឺជា $\R$-vs ។

លំហវ៉ិចទ័ររង (subspaces)

(Subspaces)

គេឱ្យ $\E$ ជា $\K$-vs និង $\mathbf W$ ជាផ្នែកមួយ (សំណុំរង) របស់ $\E$ ។ គេថា $\mathbf W$ គឺជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E$ ប្រសិនបើ

  1. $(\mathbf W,+)$ ជាក្រុមរងរបស់ $(\E,+)$
  2. $\forall \lambda \in \K, \forall x \in \mathbf W: \lambda \cdot x \in \mathbf W\quad $ (បំណិតចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $\cdot$)

ចំណាំ : បើ $\mathbf W$ ជាលំហរងរបស់ $\E$ នោះគេបាន $\mathbf W$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័រមួយ ដែលកំណត់យកប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅដូចលំហ $\E$ ដែរ គឺ៖ $$ \begin{aligned} \K \times \mathbf W & \longrightarrow \mathbf W \\ (\alpha, x) & \longmapsto \alpha \cdot x \end{aligned} $$

(Verify a subspace)
  1. សំណុំ $\mathbf W$ ជាស្វ៊ីតចំនួនពិត ដែលមានតួអនន្តខិតទៅរកសូន្យ កំណត់ដូចខាងក្រោម៖ $$ \mathbf W=\left\{\left(x_n\right)_{n \geq 0} \in \mathbb{R}^{\mathbb{N}}: \lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} x_n=0\right\} $$ ចូរបង្ហាញថា $\mathbf W$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E=\mathbb{R}^{\mathbb{N}}$ ?
    Show solution

    ចម្លើយ. ចូរបង្ហាញថា $\mathbf W$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E=\mathbb{R}^{\mathbb{N}}$

    1. បង្ហាញថា \( W \neq \varnothing \):
      ដោយស្វ៊ីត \( (0, 0, \ldots) \) រួមទៅរក \( 0 \) គេបាន៖ \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = 0 \quad (\text{ true }) \] នាំឱ្យ \( 0 \in W \) ។ ដូច្នេះ \( W \neq \varnothing \) ។
    1. យក \( x = (x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_n, \ldots) \) និង \( y = (y_0, y_1, \ldots, y_n, \ldots) \) s.t \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = 0, \quad \lim_{n \to \infty} y_n = 0 \] ដោយ \( x, y \in W \) គេបានផលបូក៖ \[ x + y = (x_0 + y_0, x_1 + y_1, \ldots, x_n + y_n, \ldots) \] តែដោយ \[\lim_{n \to \infty} (x_n + y_n) = \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n + \lim_{n \to \infty} y_n = 0 + 0 = 0 \] គេបាន \( x + y \in W \) ។ ដូច្នេះ \( W \) មានលក្ខណៈបំណិតចំពោះ $(+)$ ។
    1. បង្ហាញថា \( W \) មានលក្ខណៈបំណិតចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $(\cdot)$ (ប្រមាណវិធីគុណនឹងស្កាលែរ) :
      យក \( x = (x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_n, \ldots)\) ដែល \( \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = 0 \) និងយក \( \lambda \in \mathbb R\) ។ គេបាន៖ \[ \lambda \cdot x = (\lambda x_0, \lambda x_1, \ldots, \lambda x_n, \ldots) \] ដោយ \( \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = 0 \) នោះគេបាន៖ \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} (\lambda x_n) = \lambda \cdot \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = \lambda \cdot 0 = 0 \] ដូច្នេះ \( \lambda \cdot x \in W \) មានលក្ខណៈបំណិតចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $(\cdot)$ ។

    ដូច្នេះ $W$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $(\mathbb R^{\mathbb N}, +, \cdot)$ ។

  2. គេឱ្យ $I = (a,b)$ ឬ $[a,b]$ ជាចន្លោះមួយរបស់ $\mathbb{R}$ និងយក $\mathbf W = \mathcal{C}(I, \mathbb{R} )$ សំណុំនៃអនុគមន៍ជាប់ កំណត់ពី $I$ ទៅ $\mathbb{R}$ គឺជា $\mathbb{R}$-vs ។
    ចូរបង្ហាញថា $\mathbf W = \mathcal{C}(I, \mathbb{R})$ គឺជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E=\mathbb{R}^I$ ដែលជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័រនៃគ្រប់អនុគមន៍ទាំងអស់កំណត់ពី $I$ ទៅ $\mathbb{R}$ ។
    Show solution

    ចម្លើយ. ${}$ គេមាន ៖

    • \(I = (a,b)\) ឬ \(I = [a,b]\) ជាចន្លោះចំនួនពិត ។
    • \(\mathbf{V} = \mathbb{R}^I\) សំណុំនៃគ្រប់អនុគមន៍កំណត់ពី \(I\) ទៅ \(\mathbb{R}\) ជា $\R$-vs ។
    • \(\mathbf{W} = \mathcal{C}(I, \mathbb{R})\) ជាសំណុំនៃគ្រប់អនុគមន៍ជាប់កំណត់ពី \(I\) ទៅ \(\mathbb{R}\) ។
    យើងនឹងស្រាយបញ្ជាក់ករណីខាងក្រោម៖
    1. \(\mathbf{W} \subseteq \E\) :
      យក \(f \in \mathbf{W}\)
      តាមនិយមន័យរបស់ $\mathbf W$ គេបាន \(f: I \to \mathbb{R}\) ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់កំណត់ពីចន្លោះ $I$ ទៅ $\mathbb R$ ។ គ្រប់អនុគមន៍ជាប់ពី \(I\) ទៅ \(\mathbb{R}\) គឺជាអនុគមន៍កំណត់ពី \(I\) ទៅ \(\mathbb{R}\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \(f \in \E\) ។
    1. \(\mathbf{W}\) ផ្ទុកវ៉ិចទ័រសូន្យ :
      គេមានអនុគមន៍សូន្យ (zero function) \(\mathbf 0: I \to \mathbb{R}\) កំណត់ដោយ \(\mathbf 0(x) = 0\) ។
      គេបាន \(\mathbf 0\) ជាអនុគមន៍ថេរកំណត់លើ \(I\) ។
      ដោយអនុគមន៍ថេរជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់ នោះគេបាន \(\mathbf 0 \in \mathbf{W}\) ។
    1. \(\mathbf{W}\) បិទចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង $(+)$ :
      យក \(f, g \in \mathbf{W}\) ។ ដូច្នេះ \(f\) និង \(g\) ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់លើ \(I\) ។
      សន្មត \(x_0 \in I\) ជាធាតុទូទៅ ។
      ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(\varepsilon \gt 0\) យើងនឹងរក \(\delta \gt 0\) ដែល : \[ |x - x_0| \lt \delta \implies \Bigl|(f+g)(x) - (f+g)(x_0)\Bigr| \lt \varepsilon \]
      • តាមភាពជាប់របស់អនុគមន៍ \(f\) យក \(\varepsilon_1 = \frac{\varepsilon}{2}\) គេបាន៖ \[\exists \delta_1 \gt 0: |x - x_0| \lt \delta_1 \implies |f(x) - f(x_0)| \lt \frac{\epsilon}{2}\]
      • ដូចគ្នា ចំពោះអនុគមន៍ \(g\) យក \(\varepsilon_2 =\frac{\varepsilon}{2}\) គេបាន៖ \[\exists \delta_2 \gt 0: |x - x_0| \lt \delta_2 \implies |g(x) - g(x_0)| \lt \frac{\varepsilon}{2}\]
      ដោយយក \(\delta = \min(\delta_1, \delta_2)\) នោះបើ \(|x - x_0| \lt \delta\) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \Bigl|(f+g)(x) - (f+g)(x_0)\Bigr| \;\;&=&\;\; \Bigl|f(x) + g(x) - f(x_0) - g(x_0)\Bigr|\\ \;\; &\leq &\;\; \Bigl|f(x) - f(x_0)\Bigr| + \Bigl|g(x) - g(x_0)\Bigr| \\ \;\;&\lt &\;\; \frac{\varepsilon}{2} + \frac{\varepsilon}{2} = \varepsilon \end{eqnarray*} ដូច្នេះ \(f+g\) ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់លើ $I$ ។ ដូច្នេះ \(f+g \in \mathbf{W}\) ។
    1. \(\mathbf{W}\) បិទចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $(\cdot)$ :
      សន្មត \(f\) ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់កំណត់លើ \(I\) និងយក \(\alpha \in \R\) ។ តាង \(g(x) = (\alpha \cdot f)(x) = \alpha \cdot f(x)\) យើងនឹងបង្ហាញថាអនុគមន៍ \(g\) ជាប់ត្រង់ចំណុចទូទៅ \(x_0 \in I\) ?
      គ្រប់ \(\varepsilon \gt 0\) គេបាន៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \Bigl|g(x) - g(x_0)\Bigr| \;\;&=&\;\; \Bigl|c \cdot f(x) - c \cdot f(x_0)\Bigr| \\ \;\;&=&\;\; |c| \times \Bigl|f(x) - f(x_0)\Bigr| \end{eqnarray*}
      • ករណី \(c = 0\):
        គេបាន \(g(x) = 0\) ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(x\) ។ ដូច្នេះ $g$ ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់ ព្រោះជាអនុគមន៍ថេរ ។
      • ករណី \(c \neq 0\):
        ដោយ \(f\) ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់ត្រង់ \(x_0\) នោះគ្រប់ \(\varepsilon \gt 0\) មាន \(\delta_f \gt 0\) ដែល \[ |x - x_0| \lt \delta_f \implies |f(x) - f(x_0)| \lt \frac{\varepsilon}{|c|} \] ជ្រើសយក \(\delta = \delta_f\) នោះចំពោះ \(|x - x_0| \lt \delta\) គេបាន ៖ \begin{eqnarray*} \Bigl|g(x) - g(x_0)\Bigr| \;\;&=&\;\; |c|\times \Bigl|f(x) - f(x_0)\Bigr| \\ \;\;&\lt &\;\; |c| \times \frac{\varepsilon}{|c|} = \varepsilon \end{eqnarray*} ដូច្នេះ គ្រប់ \(\varepsilon \gt 0\) មាន \(\delta = \delta_f \gt 0\) ដែល : \[ |x - x_0| \lt \delta \implies |g(x) - g(x_0)| \lt \varepsilon \] ដូច្នេះ \(g\) ជាអនុគមន៍ជាប់ត្រង់ចំណុចទូទៅ $x_0\in I$ ។ ដូច្នេះ \(\alpha \cdot f \in \mathbf{W}\) ។
      ដូច្នេះ \(\mathbf{W} = \mathcal{C}(I, \mathbb{R})\) ជាលំហរងរបស់ \(\mathcal{E} = \mathbb{R}^I\) ។

  3. យក $\K$ ជាកាយត្រឡប់ ។ ចំពោះគ្រប់ចំនួនគត់ $n \geq 0$ គេតាង $\mathbf W = \K_n[x]$ ជាសំណុំរងរបស់ $\E=\K[x]$ កំណត់ដូចខាងក្រោម៖ $$ \K_n[x]=\Bigl\{P \in \K[x]: \operatorname{deg}(P) \leq n\Bigr\} $$
    បង្ហាញថា $\K_n[x]$ ជាលំហរងរបស់ $\K[x]$?
    Show solution
    ចម្លើយ. យក \(\K\) ជាកាយត្រឡប់ និងកំណត់ចំនួនគត់ \(n \geq 0\) ។ គេកំណត់៖ \[ \K_n[x] = \{P \in \K[x] : \operatorname{deg}(P) \leq n\} \] យើងនឹងស្រាយថា \(\K_n[x]\) ជាលំហរងរបស់ \(\K[x]\) ដោយផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់លក្ខខណ្ឌទាំងបី៖
    • Contains zero vector:
      យក \(\mathbf 0\) ជាពហុធាសូន្យ ដែលគ្រប់មេគុណទាំងអស់ស្មើសូន្យ គឺ៖ \[ 0 = 0 \cdot x^0 + 0 \cdot x^1 + 0 \cdot x^2 + \ldots + 0 \cdot x^n, \] គេបាន \(\operatorname{deg}(\mathbf 0) = -\infty\;\implies \operatorname{deg}(\mathbf 0)\leq n\) ចំពោះគ្រប់ \(n \geq 0\) ។
      ដូច្នេះ \(\mathbf 0 \in \K_n[x]\) ។
    • Closed under addition:
      យក \(P, Q \in \K_n[x]\) ។ គេបាន \[ \operatorname{deg}(P) \leq n, \quad \operatorname{deg}(Q) \leq n \] ដោយដឹងថា \[ \operatorname{deg}(P + Q) \leq \max(\operatorname{deg}(P), \operatorname{deg}(Q)) \leq n \] ដូច្នេះ \(P + Q \in \K_n[x]\) ។
    • Closed under scalar multiplication:
      យក \(P \in \K_n[x]\) និង \(\alpha \in \K\) ។ គេបាន \(\operatorname{deg}(P) \leq n\) ។ ចំពោះ \(\alpha\cdot P\) :
      • បើ \(\alpha= 0\) នោះ \(\alpha\cdot P = \mathbf 0\) ជាពហុធាសូន្យនៅក្នុង \(\K_n[x]\) ។
      • បើ \(\alpha \neq 0\) នោះ \(\operatorname{deg}(\alpha\cdot P) = \operatorname{deg}(P) \leq n\) ព្រោះ ផលគុណរវាង $\alpha$ និង \(P(x)\) កំណត់ដោយ : \[ (\alpha\cdot P)(x) = \alpha a_nx^n + \alpha a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \cdots + \alpha a_1x + \alpha a_0 \]
      ដូច្នេះ \(\alpha \cdot P \in \K_n[x]\) ។
    ដូច្នេះ \(\K_n[x]\) គឺជាលំហរងរបស់ \(\K[x]\) ។

  1. ប្រសព្វរបស់លំហរង៖ គេឱ្យ $\E$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័រកំណត់លើកាយ $\K$ ។ ដូច្នេះ

    ប្រសព្វនៃគ្រួសារលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E$ គឺជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E$ ។

    (i.e., $\bigcap_{i \in I} W_i$ ជាលំហរង ?)

    ដំណោះស្រាយ. ↩

    ចម្លើយ. ${}$ សន្មតគ្រួសារ \(\Bigl\{ W_i \Bigr\}_{i \in I}\) នៃលំហរងរបស់ \(\E\) ដែលកំណត់សន្ទស្សន៍ដោយ \(I\) ។ យើងតាងប្រសព្វនៃគ្រួសារលំហរងទាំងនេះដោយ៖ \[ W = \bigcap_{i \in I} W_i = \Bigl\{ x \in \E \mid x \in U_i \text{ for all } i \in I \Bigr\} \] យើងនឹងបង្ហាញថា \(W\) ជាលំហរងរបស់ \(\E\) !
    1. Zero vector is in \(W\):
      ដោយ \(W_i\) ជាលំហរងរបស់ \(\E\) នោះគ្រប់ \(i \in I\): \( \mathbf{0_\E} \in W_i \) ។ ដូច្នេះ \[ \mathbf{0_\E} \in W = \bigcap_{i \in I} W_i \]
    1. Closure under addition:
      យក \(u, v \in W\) ។ គេបាន \(u, v \in W_i\) គ្រប់ \(i \in I\)
      ដោយ \(W_i\) ជាលំហរងរបស់ $\E$ នោះ ៖ \[ u + v \in W_i, \quad \forall i \in I \] ដូច្នេះ \[ u + v \in W = \bigcap_{i \in I} W_i \] ដូច្នេះ \(W\) ស្តាបចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្នុង $(+)$ ។
    1. Closure under scalar multiplication:
      យក \(u \in W\) និងកំណត់ \(\alpha \in \K\) ។ តាមនិយមន័យរបស់ \(W\) ៖ \(u \in W_i\) គ្រប់ \(i \in I\) ។ ដោយ $W_i$ ជាលំហរងរបស់ $\E$ គេបាន ៖ \[ \alpha \cdot u \in W_i, \quad \forall i \in I \] ដូច្នេះ \[ \alpha \cdot u \in W = \bigcap_{i \in I} W_i \] ដូច្នេះ \(W\) ស្តាបចំពោះប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ $(\cdot)$ ។

    ដោយ \(W\) ផ្ទុកវ៉ិចទ័រសូន្យ ស្តាបចំពោះ $(+)$ និង $(\cdot)$ នោះគេបាន \(W\) គឺជាលំហរងរបស់ \(\E\) ។

  2. ប្រជុំរបស់លំហរង៖ យក $\E$ ជា $\K$-vs និងកំណត់ $U$ និង $W$ ជាពីរលំហវ៉ិចចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E$ ។ ដូច្នេះ

    $U \cup W$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E$ លុះត្រាតែ $U \subseteq W$ ឬក៏ $W \subseteq U$ ។

    ដំណោះស្រាយ. ↩

    សម្រាយបញ្ជាក់. ដើម្បីបញ្ជាក់ថា \( U \cup W \) គឺជាវ៉ិចទ័ររងនៃ \( \E \) លុះត្រាតែ \( U \subseteq W \) ឬ \( W \subseteq U \) យើងត្រូវបង្ហាញ លក្ខខណ្ឌពីរគឺ៖
    1. ល.ខ.ចាំបាច់ : យក \( U \cup W \) ជាលំហរងរបស់ \( \E \) ។
      ស្រាយថា \( U \subseteq W \) ឬ \( W \subseteq U \)? (តាមវិធីផ្ទុយពីការពិត)
      សន្មត \( U \cup W \) ជាលំហរងរបស់ \( \E \) និងកំណត់ \( U \nsubseteq W \) និង \( W \nsubseteq U \) ។ តាមន័យនេះ គេបាន៖

      \( x \in U \;\) s.t \(\; x \notin W \)
      \( y \in W \;\) s.t \(\; y \notin U \)

      ដោយ \( U \cup W \) ជាលំហរង នោះ \( x + y \in U \cup W \) ចំពោះគ្រប់ $x, y\in U \cup W$ ។ គេបាន

      \(x + y \in U \quad\) ឬ \(\quad x + y \in W \)

      • បើ \( x + y \in U \) នោះ \( y = (x + y) - x \) ។
        ដោយ \( x \in U \) និង \( U \) ជាលំហរង នោះ \( y \) ត្រូវតែជារបស់ \( U \)

    $$ \left(x_1, \cdots, x_n\right) \in \prod_{i=1}^n W_i: \;\; x=x_1+x_2+\cdots+x_n $$
    Notation: ក្នុងករណីដែលផលបូក $W_1+W_2+\cdots+W_n$ គឺជាផលបូកផ្ទាល់ នោះគេតាងវាដោយ៖ $$ W_1 \oplus W_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus W_n \quad \text { or } \quad \bigoplus_{i=1}^n W_i $$

    Solution. ↩

    សម្រាយបញ្ជាក់.
    1. $(\Longrightarrow)$: Let $\left(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n\right) \in W_1 \times W_2 \times \cdots \times W_n$, such that $x_1+x_2+\cdots+x_n=0$ . We must check that $x_1=x_2=\cdots=x_n=0$. Since the sum is direct and since we have $0=0+0+\ldots+0$, then according to the uniqueness of the decomposition, we have $x_1=x_2=\cdots=x_n=0$.
    2. $(\Longleftarrow)$: Let $x \in W_1+W_2+\cdots+W_n$, such that $x=x_1+x_2+\cdots+x_n$ and $x=y_1+y_2+\cdots+y_n$. we must then show that $x_1=y_1, x_2=y_2, \ldots, x_n=y_n$. $x=x_1+x_2+\cdots+x_n$ and $x=y_1+y_2+\cdots+y_n$, so we will have $$ \left(x_1-y_1\right)+\left(x_2-y_2\right)+\cdots+\left(x_n-y_n\right)=0 $$ Consequently, if we put $z_1=x_1-y_1, z_2=x_2-y_2, \ldots$ and $z_n=x_n-y_n$, then we will have $\left(z_1, z_2, \cdots, z_n\right) \in W_1 \times W_2 \times \ldots \times W_n$ and $z_1+z_2+\cdots+z_n=0$, so, by hypothesis, we have $ z_1=z_2=\cdots=z_n=0$.

លំហផលចែក (Quotient spaces)

A quotient vector space is a construction in linear algebra that creates a new vector space from an existing one by "collapsing" certain vectors into equivalence classes. This is done by dividing the original vector space \(\E\) by a subspace \( W \), producing the quotient vector space \( \E / W \).

(លំហផលចែក)

យក $\E$ ជា $\K$-vs និងកំណត់ $\F$ ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររងរបស់ $\E$ ។ គេតាង $\E / \F$ ជា ក្រុមផលចែក (quotient group) របស់ $\E$ តាម $\F$ ។ គេបាន $\E/\F$ គឺជា $\K$-vs ដែលមានប្រមាណវិធីក្រៅ ៖

$$ \begin{array}{rccl} \cdot : & \K \times \E /\F & \longrightarrow & \E /\F \\ &\bigl(\alpha, [x]\bigr) &\longmapsto & \alpha \cdot [x]=[\alpha \cdot x] \end{array} $$ ហៅថា លំហវ៉ិចទ័រផលចែក (quotient vector space) របស់ $\E$ តាម $\F$ (លំហវ៉ិចទ័រផលចែក $\E$ លើ $\F$) ។

ការពន្យល់៖ Let \(\E\) be a vector space over a field \(\mathbb{K}\) and let \(\F\) be a subspace of \(\E\) (i.e., \(\F \subseteq \E\)). The quotient space, denoted by \(\E/\F\), is defined as the set of equivalence classes of vectors in \(\E\) under the equivalence relation defined by the subspace \(\F\).

  • ទំនាក់ទំនងសមមូល៖ Two vectors in \(\E\) are "equivalent" if their difference belongs to the subspace \( W \), i.e., \( u, v \in V \): \[ u \sim v \iff u - v \in W \] This means that \( u \) and \( v \) are in the same equivalence class if their difference lies in \( W \).

    The set of equivalence classes \( [v] \) (where \( v \in \E\)) is defined as: \[ [v] = \{v + w \mid w \in W\} \] Each equivalence class \( [v] \) is a coset of \( W \) in \( \E\). The set of all equivalence classes forms the

    ធាតុរបស់លំហ \( \E / W \): are equivalence classes (or cosets) \( [v] = v + W \), where: \[ v + W = \Bigl\{v + w \mid w \in W\Bigr\} \] Thus, each equivalence class represents all vectors in \( \E\) that differ from \( v \) by some vector in \( W \).
  • ទម្រង់ជាលំហវ៉ិចទ័ររបស់ \(\E/ W \): លំហផលចែក \( \E / W \) បង្កើតបានជាទម្រង់លំហវ៉ិចទ័រយកចេញពីលំហ \( \E \) ដែលមានប្រមាណវិធីពីរជាចាំបាច់ កំណត់ដូចខាងក្រោម៖
    1. Internal operation (addition $+$) : For \( [u], [v] \in \E / W \), define: \[ [u] + [v] = [u + v] \]
    2. External operation (scalar multiplication $\cdot $): For \( \alpha \in \mathbb{K} \) and \( [v] \in \E / W \), define: \[ \alpha [v] = [\alpha v] \] These operations are well-defined because they do not depend on the choice of representatives from each equivalence class.

ការអនុវត្តរបស់លំហផលចែក៖ Quotient vector spaces arise in many areas of mathematics and its applications: (i) Linear Algebra: Simplifies computations by reducing dimensionality. (ii) Differential Geometry: Used in constructing tangent spaces of manifolds. (iii) Functional Analysis: Quotients of function spaces help in understanding solution spaces of differential equations. (iv) Coding Theory: Helps in analyzing vector spaces modded by a code subspace.

Philosophy: Imagine \( \E \) as a "high-dimensional space" and \( W \) as a "lower-dimensional subspace." The quotient space \( \E / W \) is the space of directions orthogonal to \( W \), effectively collapsing \( W \) to a single point (the zero vector in \( \E / W \)).

Quotient of \( \mathbb{R}^3 \) by a subspace:

Consider \( \E = \mathbb{R}^3 \) and \( W \) as the subspace of \( \mathbb{R}^3 \) consisting of all vectors of the form \( (x, 0, 0) \), i.e., the \( x \)-axis.

  1. Equivalence Classes: Two vectors \( u = (u_1, u_2, u_3) \) and \( v = (v_1, v_2, v_3) \) in \( \mathbb{R}^3 \) are equivalent if \( u - v \in W \). This means: \[ u - v = (u_1 - v_1, u_2 - v_2, u_3 - v_3) \in W \] So \( u_2 = v_2 \) and \( u_3 = v_3 \), but \( u_1 \) and \( v_1 \) can differ by any amount.
  2. Quotient Space:
    The quotient space \( \mathbb{R}^3 / W \) consists of equivalence classes of vectors that differ only in their \( x \)-component. Intuitively, \( \mathbb{R}^3 / W \) is "flattened" to \( \mathbb{R}^2 \), as the \( x \)-component is "collapsed out."

A basic method for constructing subspaces of a given vector space $\E$ is to take all linear combinations of a fixed collection of vectors in $\E$. For such a construction, we create a spanning set. A spanning set of a vector space $\E$ is a set of vectors whose linear combinations can produce every vector in that vector space. In other words, the spanning set "spans" the entire space.

(Spanning set)

យក $\E$ ជា $\K$-vs និង កំណត់ $S=\Bigl\{\vec x_i\in V \ | \, i\in I\Bigr\}$ ជាសំណុំរងរបស់ $\E$ ។

  1. We called បន្សំលីនេអ៊ែរ (linear combination) of vectors of $S$, the vector $\vec x\in \E$ such that there exists a family of scalars $\{\lambda_i\}_{i\in I}$ where only finitely many are non zero and $$ \vec x = \lambda_1 \vec x_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n \vec x_n = \sum _{i\in I}\lambda_i \cdot\vec x_i$$
  2. The span (ភាពបង្ក) (or linear closure) of a nonempty subset S of $\E$ which is denoted $\langle S \rangle$, is the set of all linear combinations of vectors from $S$, i .e. $$\bigl\langle S \bigr\rangle = \left\{\; \sum _{i\in I} \lambda_i\cdot \vec x_i : \ \lambda_i\in {\K}, \vec x_i\in S, I\subset \mathbb N\; \right\}$$
  3. We say \( S \) spans \( V \) (or $S$ is called spanning set of $\E$) if $\E = \bigl\langle S \bigr\rangle$. i.e., all vectors in the whole space $\E$ can be created from the vectors in \( S \).
Remember!
  • The set $I$ is either finite or infinite, either countable or uncountable.
  • Notice that a linear combination $ \sum _{i\in I}a_i\vec x_i$ is always a finite sum even if $S$ is infinite. The definition of a vector space gives a rule for adding two vectors, so finitely many vectors, but an infinite sum has no meaning. However, we can consider the sums $\vec s_n=\sum_{i=0}^n\vec x_i$ and the sequence $(\vec s_n)_{n\in {\N}}$. If we define a topology on the vector space, then the notion of convergence makes sense and the infinite sum can be defined. But, the vector space must be equipped with a topology, for example, an inner product space. In these lecture notes, we only consider vector space without topology.
  • Any set containing a spanning set is a spanning set.

Let $e_i$ be the series of all zeroes except the $i$-th position. The vectors $e_i$, where $i\in {\N}$, don't span ${\K}^{\N}$. Every linear combination of the $e_i$'s is a vector with finitely many non-zero components.

Thus, the vector $(1,1,\ldots,1,\ldots)\in {\K}^{\N}$ cannot be written as a linear combination of the $e_i$'s.
Important:
  1. Complete Representation: If a set of vectors spans a space \(V\), then no vector in \(V\) is left out of the linear combinations of these vectors.
  2. Minimal Spanning Set: If a spanning set is also linearly independent, it forms a basis for the vector space.
  3. Overlapping Spanning Sets: If a spanning set has more vectors than necessary, it is not linearly independent. Removing redundant vectors can result in a basis.

Let $A$ be an $n \times n$ matrix. Then the following are equivalent:

  1. $A$ is invertible.
  2. $A x = \mathbf{0}$ has only the trivial solution.
  3. The reduced row echelon form of $A$ is $I_n$.
  4. $A$ is a product of elementary matrices.
  5. $A x = b$ is consistent for every $n \times 1$ matrix $b$.
  6. $A x=b$ has exactly one solution for every $n \times 1$ matrix $\mathbf{b}$.
  7. $\operatorname{det} A \neq 0$.
The main point of the theorem is that (for square matrices) the coefficient matrix $A$ determines whether any system $A x = b$ is consistent, and knowing data about $A$ (in particular, its determinant) tells us immediately whether or not the system will be consistent.

គោល និង វិមាត្រ (Basis and Dimension)

The notions of a linearly independent set of vectors in a vector space $V$, $\mathbb K$-vs, and of a set of vectors that span $V$ were known. Any set of vectors that span $V$ can be reduced to some minimal collection of linearly independent vectors; such a set is called a basis of the subspace $V$.
(គោល និង វិមាត្រ)

Let $V$ be a $\mathbb K$-vector space, then,

  1. A set $S$ is a basis for $V$ if $S$ is linearly independent and $V=\mathrm{span}(S)$.
  2. If $S$ is a basis of $V$ and $S$ has only finitely many elements, then we say that $V$ is finite-dimensional.
  3. The number of vectors in $S$ is the dimension of $V$.

Suppose $V$ is a finite-dimensional vector space, and $S$ and $T$ are two different bases for $V$. One might worry that $S$ and $T$ have a different number of vectors; then we would have to talk about the dimension of $V$ in terms of the basis $S$ or in terms of the basis $T$. Luckily this isn't what happens. Later in this chapter, we will show that $S$ and $T$ must have the same number of vectors. This means that the dimension of a vector space is basis-independent. In fact, dimension is a very important characteristic of a vector space.

  1. លំហជួរឈរ (column space) តាងដោយ \(\mathrm{Col}(A)\) : The column space of \(A\) is the set of all linear combinations of the columns of \(A\). \[ \mathrm{Col}(A) = \Bigl\{ \mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{R}^m \mid \mathbf{y} = A\mathbf{v},\;\text{ for some }\; \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n \Bigr\} \] Thus, \(\mathrm{Col}(A)\) is the span of the columns of \(A\).
  2. លំហជួរដេក (row space) តាងដោយ \mathrm{Row}(A)\) : The row space of \(A\) is the set of all linear combinations of the rows of \(A\). \[ \mathrm{Row}(A) = \Bigl\{ \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n \mid \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}^T A \text{ for some } \mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{R}^m \Bigr\} \] Thus, \(\mathrm{Row}(A)\) is the span of the rows of \(A\).
  3. លំហសូន្យ ឬ ស្នូល (null space) តាងដោយ \(\mathrm{Null}(A)\) : The null space of \(A\) is the set of all solutions to the homogeneous equation \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\): \[ \mathrm{Null}(A) = \Bigl\{ \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n \mid A\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{0} \Bigr\} \] where, \(\mathbf{0}\) is the zero vector in \(\mathbb{R}^m\).

(Rank - Nullity)
  1. The rank of a $A(m\times n)$ is the dimension of its row space or its column space, written $$ \operatorname{rank}(A) = \operatorname{dim} \mathrm{Row}(A) = \operatorname{dim} \mathrm{Col}(A) $$
  2. The nullity of a $A$ is the dimension of the kernel (null space) of $A$, written $$ \operatorname{nullity}(A) = \operatorname{dim} \operatorname{Null}(A) $$
ទ្រឹស្ដីបទ 5. : (Dimension formula)
Let $A(m\times n)$ be a matrix in $\mathcal{M}_{m\times n}(\mathbb R)$. Then: \begin{eqnarray*} n = \dim \mathbb R^n &=& \dim \mathrm{Null}(A) + \operatorname{dim} \mathrm{Row}(A)\\ &=& \mathrm{nullity}(A) + \mathrm{rank}(A) \end{eqnarray*}

សម្រាយបញ្ជាក់. ↩

សម្រាយបញ្ជាក់.
  1. Row-reduce \( A \) to its row echelon form. Perform row operations on \( A \) to transform it into its row echelon form \( R \), which has the same row space as \( A \). Let the number of non-zero rows in \( R \) be \( r \). This \( r \) is the rank of \( A \), because the row vectors in \( R \) form a basis for \( \text{Row}(A) \). Thus: \[ \dim(\text{Row}(A)) = r, \quad \text{where } r \text{ is the rank of } A. \]
  2. Basis for \( \mathbb{R}^n \). The matrix \( A \) acts on vectors in \( \mathbb{R}^n \). For each solution \( \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n \) to \( A\mathbf{x} = 0 \), the components of \( \mathbf{x} \) can be described in terms of free variables corresponding to the columns of \( A \) that are not pivot columns (i.e., the dependent variables are determined by the pivot structure). Let the number of free variables be \( d \), where \( d \) equals the number of non-pivot columns in \( A \). Then: \[ \dim(\text{Null}(A)) = d, \quad \text{where } d \text{ is the nullity of } A. \]
  3. Relationship between rank and nullity. The rank \( r \) and nullity \( d \) of \( A \) account for all \( n \) columns of \( A \). Specifically:
    - \( r \), the number of pivot columns, corresponds to the dimension of \( \text{Row}(A) \).
    - \( d \), the number of non-pivot columns, corresponds to the dimension of \( \text{Null}(A) \). Hence: \[ n = r + d. \] Substituting the definitions of \( r \) (rank) and \( d \) (nullity), this becomes: \[ \dim(\mathbb{R}^n) = \dim(\text{Row}(A)) + \dim(\text{Null}(A)). \] This completes the proof.

(The Determinant of a Three By Three Matrix)

Let \(A\) be a \(3\times 3\) matrix. Then, \(\det \left(A\right)\) is calculated by picking a row (or column) and taking the product of each entry in that row (column) with its cofactor and adding these products together.

This process when applied to the \(i^{th}\) row (column) is known as expanding along the \(i^{th}\) row (column) as is given by \[\det \left(A\right) = a_{i1}\cdot\cof(A)_{i1} + a_{i2}\cdot\cof(A)_{i2} + a_{i3}\cdot\cof(A)_{i3}\nonumber \]

NOTE: When calculating the determinant, you can choose to expand any row or any column. Regardless of your choice, you will always get the same number which is the determinant of the matrix \(A.\) This method of evaluating a determinant by expanding along a row or a column is called Laplace Expansion or Cofactor Expansion.

(The Determinant of an \(n\times n\) Matrix)

  The Determinant of a Triangular Matrix

There is a certain type of matrix for which finding the determinant is a very simple procedure. Consider the following definition.

(Triangular Matrices)

A matrix \(A\) is upper triangular if \(a_{ij}=0\) whenever \(i\gt j\). Thus the entries of such a matrix below the main diagonal equal \(0\), as shown. Here, \(\ast\) refers to any nonzero number. \[ \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} \ast & \ast & \cdots & \ast \\ 0 & \ast & \cdots & \vdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \ast \\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & \ast \end{array} \right]\nonumber \] A lower triangular matrix is defined similarly as a matrix for which all entries above the main diagonal are equal to zero.

Theorem 2: (Determinant of a Triangular Matrix)

Let \(A\) be an upper or lower triangular matrix. Then \(\det \left( A\right)\) is obtained by taking the product of the entries on the main diagonal.

The verification of this Theorem can be done by computing the determinant using Laplace Expansion along the first row or column.

(Determinant of a Triangular Matrix)

Show solution

Without using Theorem \({2}\), you could use Laplace Expansion. We will expand along the first column. (Basic definition)

Let $A(n\times n) = (a_{ij})$, the determinant is defined by the sum \begin{align*} \det(A) & = \sum_{\sigma \in S_n} \text{sign} (\sigma) a_{1\sigma(1)} a_{2\sigma(2)} \dotsc a_{n\sigma(n)} \\ & = \sum_{\sigma \in S_n} \text{sign}(\sigma) \prod_{i=1}^n a_{i\sigma(i)} \end{align*} where $S_n$ is the set of all permutations on the set $\{ 1, \dotsc, n\}$, and $\text{sign}(\sigma)$ is the parity of the permutation $\sigma$.

  2. Multiply a row by a nonzero number \((\lambda\cdot R_i^{\text{old}} \longrightarrow R_i^{\text{new}})\)

We will now consider the effect of row operations on the determinant of a matrix. In future sections, we will see that using the following properties can greatly assist in finding determinants. Properties I: (the effect of row operations)
  1. Switching Rows: យកម៉ាទ្រីស $A$ មានលំដាប់ $n\times n$ និងយក $B$ ជាម៉ាទ្រីសដែលបានមកពីការប្ដូរជួរដេកពីររបស់ម៉ាទ្រីស $ A$ ។ ដូច្នេះ គេបាន $$\det(B)=-\det(A)$$
  2. Multiplying a Row by a Scalar: បើម៉ាទ្រីស $A$ មានលំដាប់ $n\times n$ និង $B$ ជាម៉ាទ្រីសដែលបានមក ដោយគុណជួរដេកណាមួយរបស់់ម៉ាទ្រីស $A$ ជាមួយនឹង ស្កាលែរ $k\neq 0$ ។ ដូច្នេះ គេបាន $$\det(B)=k\cdot\det(A)$$
  3. Scalar Multiplication: យក \(A\) និង \(B\) ជាម៉ាទ្រីសលំដាប់ \(n \times n\) និងកំណត់ \(k\) ជាស្កាលែរមួយ ដែល \(B = k\cdot A\) ។ ដូច្នេះ គេបាន \[\det(B) = k^n\cdot \det(A)\]
  4. Adding a Multiple of a Row to Another Row: Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix and let \(B\) be a matrix which results from adding a multiple of a row to another row. Then \[\det \left( A\right) =\det \left( B \right)\]

There are several other major properties of determinants which do not involve row (or column) operations. The first is the determinant of a product of matrices.

Properties II: (major properties of determinants)
  1. Determinant of a Product: Let \(A\) and \(B\) be two \(n\times n\) matrices. Then \[\det \left( AB\right) =\det \left( A\right) \det \left( B\right)\nonumber \]
  2. Determinant of the Transpose: Let \(A\) be a matrix where \(A^T\) is the transpose of \(A\). Then, \[\det\left(A^T\right) = \det \left( A \right)\nonumber \]
  3. Determinant of the Inverse: Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix. Then \(A\) is invertible if and only if \(\det(A) \neq 0\). If this is true, it follows that \[\det(A^{-1}) = \frac{1}{\det(A)}\nonumber \]

We will see some important proofs on determinants and cofactors. First we recall the definition of a determinant.

If \(A=\left[ a_{ij} \right]\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix, then \(\det A\) is defined by computing the expansion along the first row: \begin{equation} \label{E1} \det(A) =\sum_{i=1}^n a_{1,i} \cdot \cof(A)_{1,i} \end{equation} If \(n=1\) then \(\det(A) =a_{1,1}\).

Many of the proofs in section use the Principle of Mathematical Induction. First we check that the assertion is true for \(n=2\) (the case \(n=1\) is either completely trivial or meaningless).

Next, we assume that the assertion is true for \(n-1\) (where \(n\geq 3\)) and prove it for \(n\). Once this is accomplished, by the Principle of Mathematical Induction we can conclude that the statement is true for all \(n\times n\) matrices for every \(n\geq 2\).

If \(A\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix and \(1\leq j \leq n\), then the matrix obtained by removing \(1\)st column and \(j\)th row from \(A\) is an \(n-1\times n-1\) matrix (we shall denote this matrix by \(A(j)\) below).   1. If \(A\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix such that one of its rows consists of zeros, then \(\det(A)=0\).

    Let $n\geq 3$ be such that every matrix of size $n-1\times n-1$ with a row consisting of zeros has determinant equal to zero. Let $i$ be such that the $i$th row of $A$ consists of zeros. Then we have $a_{ij}=0$ for $1\leq j \leq n$.

    Fix $j\in \{1,2,\ldots, n\}$ such that $ j\neq i$ . Then matrix $A(j)$ used in computation of $ \cof(A)_{1j}$ has a row consisting of zeros, and by our inductive assumption $\cof(A)_{1,j}=0$.

  2. Assume \(A\), \(B\) and \(C\) are \(n\times n\) matrices that for some \(1\leq i\leq n\) satisfy the following.
    1. \(j\)th rows of all three matrices are identical, for \(j\neq i\).
    2. Each entry in the \(j\)th row of \(A\) is the sum of the corresponding entries in \(j\)th rows of \(B\) and \(C\).

Suppose that values of \(x\) and corresponding values of \(y\) are given, such that the actual relationship between \(x\) and \(y\) is unknown. Then, values of \(y\) can be estimated using an interpolating polynomial \(p(x)\). If given \(x_1, ..., x_n\) and the corresponding \(y_1, ..., y_n\), the procedure to find \(p(x)\) is as follows:
  1. The desired polynomial \(p(x)\) is given by \[p(x) = r_0 + r_1 x + r_2 x^2 + ... + r_{n-1}x^{n-1}\nonumber \]
  2. \(p(x_i) = y_i\) for all \(i = 1, 2, ...,n\) so that \[\begin{array}{c} r_0 + r_1x_1 + r_2 x_1^2 + ... + r_{n-1}x_1^{n-1} = y_1 \\ r_0 + r_1x_2 + r_2 x_2^2 + ... + r_{n-1}x_2^{n-1} = y_2 \\ \vdots \\ r_0 + r_1x_n + r_2 x_n^2 + ... + r_{n-1}x_n^{n-1} = y_n \end{array}\nonumber \]
  3. Set up the augmented matrix of this system of equations \[\left[ \begin{array}{rrrrr|r} 1 & x_1 & x_1^2 & \cdots & x_1^{n-1} & y_1 \\ 1 & x_2 & x_2^2 & \cdots & x_2^{n-1} & y_2 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & &\vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & x_n & x_n^2 & \cdots & x_n^{n-1} & y_n \\ \end{array} \right]\nonumber \]
  4. Solving this system will result in a unique solution \(r_0, r_1, \cdots, r_{n-1}\). Use these values to construct \(p(x)\), and estimate the value of \(p(a)\) for any \(x=a\).

REMARK: Given \(n\) data points \((x_1, y_1), (x_2, y_2), \cdots, (x_n, y_n)\) with the \(x_i\) distinct, there is a unique polynomial \(p(x) = r_0 + r_1x + r_2x^2 + \cdots + r_{n-1}x^{n-1}\) such that \(p(x_i) = y_i\) for \(i=1,2,\cdots, n\). The resulting polynomial \(p(x)\) is called the interpolating polynomial for the data points.

Outcomes

In this section, we will work with the entire set of complex numbers, denoted by \(\mathbb{C}\). Recall that the real numbers, \(\mathbb{R}\) are contained in the complex numbers, so the discussions in this section apply to both real and complex numbers.

(Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors)
Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix and let \(x \in \mathbb{C}^{n}\) be a nonzero vector for which \[Ax=\lambda x \label{eigen1}\] for some scalar \(\lambda\). Then
  1. \(\lambda\) is called an eigenvalue of the matrix \(A\) and \(x\) is called an eigenvector of \(A\) associated with \(\lambda\), or a \(\lambda\)-eigenvector of \(A\).
  2. The set of all eigenvalues of an \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\) is denoted by \(\sigma \left( A\right)\) and is called the spectrum of \(A.\)

NOTE: The eigenvectors of a matrix \(A\) are those vectors \(X\) for which multiplication by \(A\) results in a vector in the same direction or opposite direction to \(X\). Since the zero vector \(0\) has no direction this would make no sense for the zero vector. As noted above, \(0\) is never allowed to be an eigenvector.
The expression \(\det \left( \lambda I-A\right)\) is a polynomial (in the variable \(x\)) called the characteristic polynomial of \(A\), and \(\det \left( \lambda I-A\right) =0\) is called the characteristic equation. For this reason we may also refer to the eigenvalues of \(A\) as characteristic values, but the former is often used for historical reasons.

Theorem 1. : (The Existence of an Eigenvector)

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix and suppose \[\det \left( \lambda I - A\right) =0\] for some \(\lambda \in \mathbb{C}\). Then \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\) and thus there exists a nonzero vector \(x \in \mathbb{C}^{n}\) such that \[Ax=\lambda x\]

(Multiplicity of an Eigenvalue)

Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix with characteristic polynomial given by \(\det \left( \lambda I - A\right)\). Then, the multiplicity of an eigenvalue \(\lambda\) of \(A\) is the number of times \(\lambda\) occurs as a root of that characteristic polynomial.

For my-example, suppose the characteristic polynomial of \(A\) is given by \(\left( \lambda - 2 \right)^2\). Solving for the roots of this polynomial, we set \(\left( \lambda - 2 \right)^2 = 0\) and solve for \(\lambda \). We find that \(\lambda = 2\) is a root that occurs twice. Hence, in this case, \(\lambda = 2\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\) of multiplicity equal to \(2\).
