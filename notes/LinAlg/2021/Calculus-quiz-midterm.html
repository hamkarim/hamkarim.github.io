V. Limits pass through continuous functions (Topic 5.3, page 14). Compute \[ \lim_{x \to a} f(g(x)) = f\left( \lim_{x \to a} g(x) \right) \]

  1. Let \( f(x) = \sin x \) (which is continuous everywhere) and \( g(x) = \dfrac{\pi x}{2} \), \(x \to 1\) .
  2. Given \( f(x) = e^x \) (continuous everywhere) and \( g(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} \), $x \to 1$.
  3. Let \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \) (continuous for \( x \geq 0 \)) and \( g(x) = x^2 + 4 \), $x\to -2$ .
  4. Consider \( f(x) = \ln x \) (continuous for \( x \gt 0 \)) and \( g(x) = \frac{x - 1}{x^2 - 1} \) (for \( x \neq 1 \)), $x \to 1$
  5. Let \( f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x} \) (continuous for \( x \neq 0 \)) and \( g(x) = \dfrac{\sin x}{x} \). Evaluate: \[ \lim_{x \to 0} f(g(x)) = f\left( \lim_{x \to 0} g(x) \right) \] Does the limit exist?
